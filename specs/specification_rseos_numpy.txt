"""
Find the mathematical function skeleton that represents the pressure-volume
relationship in a compressed solid, given data on volume V.

When a solid material is compressed under extreme pressure — such as minerals
in the Earth's deep interior or metals in diamond anvil cell experiments —
its volume decreases and pressure rises nonlinearly. The relationship between
pressure and volume is governed by the material's elastic properties, which
themselves change with compression.

Key physical intuitions to consider:
  - At the reference (zero-pressure) volume, pressure is zero.
  - As volume decreases below the reference, pressure rises steeply.
  - The bulk modulus (resistance to compression) scales as a power law
    with the compression ratio V/V0.
  - The leading pressure term is proportional to a strain variable built
    from the cube root of V/V0, which vanishes at the reference volume.
  - Nonlinear stiffening is captured by an exponential in that strain variable.
  - The elastic properties of the material evolve continuously throughout
    the compression range.
  - V is the only input variable.
"""

            'K_inf'   : 0.8487,
